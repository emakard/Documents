Site Information for Accessibility

Way Finding (NBC 13.2.42)
System Description
• Information to assist navigation through built environment
Key Accessibility Issues
Accessible Entrance Information
1. Is there a clear, visible International Symbol of Accessibility (ISA) at all accessible entrances?
2. Is tactile guidance provided from drop-off points to the main entrance?
3. Is the entrance free from any level differences greater than 150mm without proper warning?
4. Are all entrance signs mounted at a height between 900mm and 1800mm?
5. Is there sufficient lighting (minimum specified lux level) at the entrance area?
6. Is there a tactile layout map available at the entrance with 40% LRV contrast?
Accessible Building Usage
1. Are all emergency evacuation routes clearly marked with both visual and tactile indicators?
2. Is there a clear accessible path from entrance to all main facilities?
3. Do all level changes have appropriate TGSI warnings and 70% color contrast?
4. Are guardrails installed where level differences exceed 300mm?
5. Are all corridors free from obstacles and meet minimum width requirements?
6. Is there sufficient turning space (1500mm) at decision points?
Easy Information Access
1. Is the reception/information desk clearly visible from the entrance?
2. Are information counters at accessible heights (750mm-800mm)?
3. Is there a building directory available at the main entrance?
4. Does the directory include a QR code for digital access?
5. Are all information signs in high contrast (70% minimum)?
6. Is braille information provided at all key decision points?
Multi-Sensory Communication Methods
1. Are all important messages conveyed through at least two sensory means?
2. Is there an audio description service available?
3. Are visual alarms (strobe lights) installed for emergency communication?
4. Is there a permanently manned position for emergency communications?
5. Are all communication systems accessible from a wheelchair height?
6. Are signs updated regularly when locations/departments change?
Vision and Vision Zone (NBC B-1.3)
Signage Design (NBC B-24.2)
1. Are text and symbols a minimum of 15mm in height for key signage?
2. Is there a minimum luminance contrast of 70% between text/symbols and background?
3. Are pictograms accompanied by equivalent text description?
4. Are signs illuminated to at least 200 lux where required?
5. Is the International Symbol of Accessibility (ISA) used appropriately on accessible facilities?
6. Do directional signs include clear arrows and symbols?
Panel Placement (NBC B-24.2.4)
1. Are wall-mounted signs placed between 900mm and 1800mm from floor level?
2. Are signs in crowded areas mounted at minimum 2100mm height?
3. Is tactile information placed between 1200-1600mm from floor level?
4. Are directional signs placed at decision points and intervals of max 30m?
5. Is clear floor space of 900mm × 1200mm provided in front of tactile signs?
6. Are overhead signs mounted minimum 2100mm from floor level?
TGSI Requirements
Level Changes & Safety
• No single steps allowed
• 70% visual contrast required
• Warning for edges >150mm
• Barriers required for drops >300mm
